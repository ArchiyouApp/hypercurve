Hypercurve implementation references
====================================

Yap, Chee K. "Towards Exact Geometric Computation." Computational Geometry
7(1-2), 1997. https://doi.org/10.1016/0925-7721(95)00040-2
  Separate exact objects, predicates, and constructions. Retain replayable
  evidence and return typed blockers instead of allowing approximations to
  decide topology.

Shewchuk, Jonathan Richard. "Adaptive Precision Floating-Point Arithmetic and
Fast Robust Geometric Predicates." Discrete & Computational Geometry 18, 1997.
https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~quake/robust.html
  Filters may accelerate sign decisions, but exact fallback remains the
  topology authority.

CGAL Arrangement_on_surface_2 User Manual.
https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Arrangement_on_surface_2/index.html
  Keep curve geometry separate from arrangement topology, decompose into
  monotone spans, retain curve history, and normalize event ordering.

CGAL 2D Regularized Boolean Set Operations User Manual.
https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Boolean_set_operations_2/index.html
  Implement regularized region Booleans as arrangement overlay plus exact face
  labeling and shared-boundary ownership.

de Berg, Mark, et al. Computational Geometry: Algorithms and Applications.
Springer.
  Use sweep-line and exact bounding-box broad phases to reduce candidate pairs
  without allowing broad-phase arithmetic to decide intersections.

Sederberg, Thomas W., and Tomoyuki Nishita. "Curve Intersection Using Bezier
Clipping." Computer-Aided Design 22(9), 1990.
  Use Bernstein bounds, clipping, subdivision, and certified root isolation for
  free-form curve pairs before generic algebraic materialization.

Farin, Gerald. Curves and Surfaces for Computer-Aided Geometric Design.
Academic Press.
  Baseline for Bernstein, rational Bezier, B-spline, NURBS, knot insertion,
  degree elevation, and projective parameterization identities.

Patrikalakis, Nicholas M., Takashi Maekawa, and Woojin Cho. Shape
Interrogation for Computer Aided Design and Manufacturing.
https://web.mit.edu/hyperbook/Patrikalakis-Maekawa-Cho/
  Retain exact NURBS span extraction and global-to-local parameter mappings;
  use interval/root evidence at topology boundaries.

Hormann, Kai, and Alexander Agathos. "The Point in Polygon Problem for
Arbitrary Polygons." Computational Geometry 20(3), 2001.
  Treat boundary hits and winding classification explicitly and cache prepared
  repeated-query state.

Greiner, Gunther, and Kai Hormann. "Efficient Clipping of Arbitrary Polygons."
ACM Transactions on Graphics 17(2), 1998.

Foster, Erich L., Kai Hormann, and Romeo Traian Popa. "Clipping Simple Polygons
with Degenerate Intersections." Computers & Graphics 48, 2015.
  Use polygon clippers as degeneracy and traversal regression references, not
  as tolerance-driven topology policy.
